Detailed Findings

Section-by-Section Analysis

1. Message Match & Clarity
3.5 / 10 ▼
📊 Data signal: Homepage bounce rate is 56.3% on 3,469 sessions. Valentine's hero copy is still live in March. The /sxsw landing page (1,057 sessions) has a 92.3% bounce — highest of all top pages — indicating a severe message-match failure for event-driven traffic.
✓ Working
  • Navigation is well-organized by category (Med Spa, Luxe, Wellness, Salon)
  • Austin geo-targeting is clear throughout
  • March Specials now live in the nav (good seasonal awareness)
✗ Issues
  • Hero headline is "Valentine's Glow Starts Here" — it's March 16
  • Subheadline "Transforming Beauty, Empowering Confidence" is generic, 0 differentiation
  • "Holiday Specials" CTA still in hero section — creates trust erosion
  • /sxsw page: 1,057 sessions, zero booking CTA, 0 conversions — message dead end
  • Browser tab says "Transforming Beauty Austin" but brand is LVS / La Vie — identity fragmentation
Testable Hypothesis
IF we replace the seasonal hero with an evergreen benefit-driven headline ("Austin's Premier Med Spa — Voted #1, Two Locations") and remove stale CTAs, THEN homepage bounce rate will decrease from 56.3% BECAUSE visitors can't quickly answer "Am I in the right place?" with outdated seasonal copy.
2. Trust & Credibility
5.0 / 10 ▼
📊 Data signal: "la vie med spas & salon austin reviews" has a 5.3% CTR at position 3.1 in GSC — people are actively seeking reviews. The about-us page gets 54 sessions with 10 conversions (18.5% CVR) — high trust signals on that page work. The homepage testimonials section exists but is below the fold.
✓ Working
  • "Voted #1 Med Spa in Austin" stated in testimonial section
  • Real named testimonials with specific services mentioned
  • Woman-owned business mentioned — relevant differentiator for ICP
  • Real phone number and address in footer
✗ Issues
  • "Voted #1 Med Spa in Austin" buried 60%+ down the page — not in hero
  • No Google star rating badge near Book Now CTA
  • Oakland Ave location incomplete: "509 Oakland Ave." — no city, state, zip, no phone
  • No review count displayed anywhere on page
  • Testimonials have no dates — recency matters for trust
Testable Hypothesis
IF we add a Google Reviews widget (star rating + count) adjacent to the primary "Book Now" CTA and move "Voted #1 Med Spa in Austin" into the hero subtext, THEN booking conversion rate will increase BECAUSE local service buyers require trust signals at the point of commitment (Cialdini: social proof).
3. CTA & Conversion Path
3.5 / 10 ▼
📊 Data signal: Homepage CVR is 7.7% (267/3,469) but bounce rate is 56.3% — meaning a huge portion never engage with CTAs at all. /sxsw gets 1,057 sessions with 0 conversions and has no booking path. /promotions gets 350 sessions, 74.9% bounce, only 3 conversions — competing CTAs confuse visitors.
✓ Working
  • "Book Now" CTA is present and links to Mindbody
  • Gift card CTA exists for gifting intent visitors
✗ Issues
  • Hero has 3 competing CTAs: "our services," "Book Now," "holiday specials"
  • "Holiday Specials" links to /promotions but it's March — erodes trust
  • /sxsw page: no CTA, no booking link, no redirect — 1,057 sessions abandoned
  • /promotions: 350 sessions, 74.9% bounce — no clear primary CTA on page
  • CTA copy "our services" is passive — doesn't drive commitment
  • Booking goes off-site to Mindbody with no pre-selection — extra friction
Testable Hypothesis
IF we reduce hero CTAs to one primary ("Book Now") + one secondary ("View Specials") and add a booking button + embedded widget to /sxsw, THEN conversion rate on both pages will increase BECAUSE reducing choice eliminates decision paralysis (Hick's Law) and the SXSW page currently has no conversion path at all.
4. Organic Social Traffic Quality
4.0 / 10 ▼
📊 Data signal: Organic Social = 882 sessions, 11.6% engagement rate, 22s avg duration, 1.14 pages/session. This is the worst channel by engagement quality. Compare to Organic Search: 65.4% engagement, 232s. Social visitors are arriving at pages that don't match their intent from the post.
✓ Working
  • Active on Instagram + Facebook — driving meaningful traffic volume
  • Social traffic is 3rd-largest channel at 882 sessions
✗ Issues
  • 11.6% engagement rate — 88% of social visitors leave immediately
  • 22s avg duration — no time to read any content
  • Social posts likely link to homepage, not specific service/offer pages
  • No Instagram link-in-bio dedicated landing page
  • No social-specific offer or CTA to capture social intent
Testable Hypothesis
IF we create a dedicated Instagram/social landing page (/welcome or via Linktree alternative) matching the visual language of social posts with a specific offer CTA, THEN Organic Social engagement rate will increase from 11.6% BECAUSE current post → homepage routing destroys message match for social visitors.
5. Mobile Experience
5.5 / 10 ▼
📊 Data signal: Mobile = 59.8% of all sessions (3,836) but only 4.95% CVR vs 7.27% desktop CVR. That's a 32% conversion rate gap. If mobile converted at desktop rate, the site would add ~88 additional conversions over this 90-day period from the same traffic.
✓ Working
  • Site is responsive and renders on mobile
  • Phone number in footer is clickable (tel: link assumed)
✗ Issues
  • 32% mobile-to-desktop CVR gap is significant and data-confirmed
  • Hero with decorative animated icons (lips, hearts, roses) likely heavy on mobile
  • Navigation mega-menu is complex — mobile UX unclear
  • Off-site Mindbody booking increases mobile drop-off risk
  • No sticky "Book Now" button on mobile scroll
Testable Hypothesis
IF we add a sticky mobile "Book Now" button and simplify the mobile hero to a single CTA, THEN mobile CVR will improve from 4.95% toward desktop's 7.27% BECAUSE 59.8% of traffic is mobile and currently loses 32% conversion efficiency vs desktop.

Estimated impact: Closing even half the CVR gap on mobile traffic adds ~44 conversions over 90 days from existing traffic.
6. Search Intent & Commercial Keyword Gap
4.0 / 10 ▼
📊 Data signal: "med spa austin" gets 948 impressions at avg position 20.6 with only 3 clicks (0.3% CTR). "lip fillers austin" gets 393 impressions at position 85.5 — not on page 1. The homepage ranks for 20,963 impressions but at avg position 21.3. ~75% of all GSC clicks are brand queries (la vie, vickmay). Commercial intent keywords are barely showing.
✓ Working
  • Strong brand recognition — "la vie en rainey" at position 2.2, 36.8% CTR
  • /liquid-lipo page gets 32 clicks, 3,558 impressions — on the radar
  • Event space page: 14 clicks, 1,587 impressions, position 8.3 — near page 1
✗ Issues
  • "med spa austin" (948 impr.) only 0.3% CTR at position 20 — not on page 1
  • "lip fillers austin" (393 impr.) at position 85 — not ranking at all
  • Homepage at avg position 21.3 for 20,963 impressions — huge CTR opportunity
  • No page targeting "botox austin" despite clear demand
  • avg CTR of 1.65% is below local services benchmark (~4-6%)
Testable Hypothesis
IF we optimize the homepage title tag and H1 for "med spa austin" and build dedicated landing pages for high-impression keywords (botox, lip fillers, liquid lipo), THEN organic clicks will increase from 878 over 90 days BECAUSE the site has 53,095 impressions but converts them at only 1.65% CTR — a positioning problem, not a visibility problem.

Methodology

How This Audit Was Conducted

Data Sources

GA4 (90-day period: Dec 16, 2025 – Mar 16, 2026) via service account pull. Google Search Console (same 90-day window). Live homepage crawl (March 16, 2026). ICE confidence scores: 7–9 for data-backed findings, 4–6 for heuristic-only.

CRO Frameworks Applied

Conversion Equation: Motivation − Friction − Anxiety + Incentive. Visitor 5 Questions. Hick's Law (choice reduction). Cialdini's social proof. Fogg Behavior Model. Decision Design — System 1 optimization first.

Section Weights

Local Services profile applied: Trust & Credibility 25%, Message Match 20%, CTA Path 15%, Value Prop 15%, Form/Booking UX 10%, Content/Persuasion 10%, Mobile 5%.

ICE Scoring

Impact (1–10) × Confidence (1–10) × Ease (1–10) averaged to ICE Score. Confidence is capped at 9 for data-backed recommendations and 6 for heuristic-only. Sortable in the backlog table above.

Business Context

Local med spa, two Austin locations. Primary goal: booking conversions via Mindbody. ICP: Austin women seeking luxury beauty and wellness services. SXSW seasonal traffic surge confirmed in data.

Next Steps

After quick wins: set up conversion event tracking in GA4 to properly measure booking CVR. Implement heatmap (Hotjar or Microsoft Clarity) to confirm mobile UX friction points. A/B test hero headline with Google Optimize or VWO.
