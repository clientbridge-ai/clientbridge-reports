Detailed Findings

5 Message Match & Clarity

▾

What's Working

Headline identifies the target audience (Loan Officers) and the value (recession-proof professionals).
"Endorsed by" logo bar provides immediate industry context — visitor knows they're in the right vertical.

Issues Found

Hero headline is long and complex: "GO! Coaching Combines the Power of Loan Officers and Digital Marketing Experts to Build Recession-Proof Professionals." This requires System 2 processing — too many concepts jammed into one sentence for a 3-second scan.
Sub-headline "Feeling Stuck With Outdated Tactics? GO! for a Change" is clever but vague — doesn't tell the visitor what they'll specifically get or what "a change" means.
The page serves dual audiences (Loan Officers AND Real Estate Agents) with a dropdown navigation choice, creating a cognitive fork at the very top of the funnel. Visitors arriving from targeted campaigns will encounter content not meant for them.
Cart/merch UI (shopping cart with "Astro Tee" placeholder items) is visible and clutters the navigation, detracting from the primary conversion goal.

Recommendations

Simplify the hero headline to one clear benefit: e.g., "Become a $1M+ Loan Officer in 6 Months" — short, specific, measurable.
Create dedicated landing pages per audience (Loan Officer vs Agent) and route paid traffic directly to the relevant page, bypassing the homepage fork entirely.
Remove or minimize the merch cart icon from the primary navigation — it competes with the conversion CTA.
IF we shorten the hero headline to ≤10 words focused on a single measurable outcome
THEN homepage engagement rate will increase for paid traffic visitors
BECAUSE shorter headlines reduce cognitive load and answer "Am I in the right place?" within 3 seconds (System 1 processing)

6 Value Proposition

▾

What's Working

"Programs Built By Million-Dollar Earners" is a strong credibility-backed value prop — it answers "why you?" with proof.
The "each coach earns $1M+ a year and averages 45 hours a week" stat is specific and compelling — it addresses both income and lifestyle aspirations.
The four pillars framework (Marketing, Sales, Operations, Finance) communicates breadth of coaching coverage.

Issues Found

The "360° network alliance" language is abstract. Visitors don't know what a "360° network alliance" delivers in practical terms — leads? referrals? systems?
No pricing or program tier information anywhere on the homepage. High-ticket coaching buyers need at least a range to self-qualify before booking a call. The absence creates anxiety (Conversion Equation: +Anxiety = −Conversion).
The "Recession-Proof" claim in the page title and hero is not substantiated with data — no stats on member retention during downturns, no case studies showing revenue during 2023-2024 rate increases.

Recommendations

Add a program comparison section with tier names, key inclusions, and starting price ranges (e.g., "Starting at $X/month") to reduce anxiety and let visitors self-qualify.
Replace "360° network alliance" with specific outcomes: "Get warm referrals from 1,000+ active real estate agents in the GO! network."
Substantiate the "recession-proof" claim with at least 2-3 specific data points — member income during rate hikes, retention rate, etc.
IF we add a visible program tier comparison with starting price ranges
THEN strategy call booking rate will increase
BECAUSE price transparency reduces anxiety for high-ticket buyers and filters out unqualified leads, improving both conversion rate and lead quality

7 Trust & Credibility

▾

What's Working

Video testimonials with real names (Javier Garcia, Micah Filipsic, Chris Grano) and 5-star ratings are excellent social proof — video is the highest-trust testimonial format.
Industry endorsement logos (MBS Highway, MyHomeIQ, ShapeCRM, 4C) build vertical credibility.
Coach profiles with specific credentials ($1B+ funded, $560M+ funded, specific market rankings) are compelling authority signals.
Sherrie Liebert quote ("41 years in the mortgage business... reinventing myself") is powerful — shows even veterans find value.

Issues Found

The "4.9 out of 5 based on 100+ happy students reviews" at the bottom of the page is buried below the fold. This is the single strongest trust signal and should be above the fold.
No specific outcome metrics in testimonials — how much did revenue increase? How many loans closed after joining? Testimonials say "loving it" but don't quantify results.
No Google Business Profile or third-party review links — all social proof is self-hosted, which reduces perceived objectivity.

Recommendations

Move the "4.9/5 from 100+ students" badge to the hero section, directly under the primary CTA.
Add 2-3 outcome-focused testimonial snippets with specific numbers: "Went from $X to $Y in Z months" format.
Link to or embed Google Reviews / Trustpilot for third-party validation.
IF we move the aggregate rating badge (4.9/5, 100+ reviews) to directly below the hero CTA
THEN CTA click-through rate will increase
BECAUSE proximity of social proof to the conversion action reduces anxiety at the exact decision moment (trust + CTA coupling)

4 CTA & Conversion Path

▾

What's Working

Multiple CTA placements throughout the page — hero, mid-page, and bottom — ensure visibility.
CTA language "Book a Free Strategy Call" communicates zero risk.

Issues Found

Critical: The primary CTA "Book a Free Strategy Call" links to a "#" anchor — it does not navigate to a booking page, Calendly embed, or any functional conversion mechanism. If this is truly broken, it's a zero-conversion scenario for every visitor who clicks it.
Multiple competing CTAs on the same viewport: "Book a Free Strategy Call," "Get Started," "Get Growing," "Let's Talk," and "Member Login" all fight for attention. The page violates the one-primary-CTA-per-viewport principle.
The 3-step process (Schedule → Consultation → Get Growing) is good in concept but doesn't have clickable/actionable steps — Step 1 should BE the booking widget or link.
"WATCH THIS" video CTA competes with the booking CTA in the hero area, splitting visitor attention between consuming content and converting.
The "Creativ" program card links to "#" — another dead end that creates a broken user experience.

Recommendations

Urgent fix: Ensure all "Book a Call" CTAs link to a functional Calendly, HubSpot, or native booking page. This is the single highest-impact fix.
Consolidate CTA language to a single phrase site-wide. Pick "Book a Free Strategy Call" and use it everywhere — remove "Get Started," "Get Growing," and "Let's Talk" as they dilute the primary action.
Embed a Calendly widget directly on the page (or at minimum, as an in-page modal) so visitors never leave the site to book.
Add a secondary CTA for lower-intent visitors: "Download our Free Guide" or "Watch the Free Training" to capture leads who aren't ready to talk.
IF we fix all CTA links to route to a functional booking page and consolidate to one CTA phrase
THEN strategy call bookings will increase dramatically (estimated 5-15x if currently broken)
BECAUSE a non-functional primary CTA means zero conversions — fixing it removes a total conversion blocker

5 Form / Booking UX

▾

What's Working

The newsletter signup form at the bottom is simple — Name, Email, and a dropdown — minimal friction for a secondary conversion.
Newsletter form includes a trust statement: "We will NEVER spam you or sell your information."

Issues Found

The primary booking flow (strategy call) has no visible form, modal, or embedded widget on the page. Visitors click a CTA and get nothing — there's no form to evaluate because the conversion path appears broken.
The newsletter dropdown "What describes you best?" defaults to "Mortgage Advisor" with no visible alternative options — it may not serve the Real Estate Agent audience at all.
No lead magnet or value exchange beyond "newsletter" — visitors who aren't ready to book a call have very limited options to engage.

Recommendations

Implement an embedded Calendly or HubSpot Meetings widget that appears either inline on the page or as a modal when CTA is clicked.
Add a mid-funnel lead capture: free training video, downloadable playbook, or assessment quiz (e.g., "Rate Your Digital Marketing Maturity") in exchange for email.
Add qualifying questions to the booking form (annual volume, biggest challenge) to improve lead quality and enable personalized follow-up.
IF we embed a booking widget directly on the page with 2-3 qualifying questions
THEN both booking rate and lead quality will improve
BECAUSE reducing navigation steps to book (from click → new page → select time) to (click → modal → select time) eliminates one full drop-off point

6 Content & Persuasion

▾

What's Working

The "How do we fuel your growth?" section with 7 benefit cards (Mentorship, Sustainable Growth, Consultations, Strategies, Community, Marketing, Alliance) covers key buyer concerns comprehensively.
FAQ section addresses common objections: speed of results, coaching format, and career progression ("Can I be a coach one day?").
The 3-step process creates a clear mental model of the buyer journey.

Issues Found

No objection handling around price — the #1 objection for coaching programs. FAQs address what coaching includes but never touch on investment level, ROI expectations, or payment flexibility.
No case study or "before/after" narrative — the page has testimonials but no detailed transformation story with specific numbers and timeline.
The page is very long with repetitive sections — "Your growth begins three ways" (Classroom, Community, Coaching) essentially repeats the benefit cards above it. This dilutes impact.
Urgency and scarcity are completely absent — no cohort dates, no limited spots, no enrollment deadlines. For high-ticket coaching, some form of real urgency drives action.

Recommendations

Add a "What's the Investment?" FAQ that addresses pricing philosophy even if exact numbers aren't listed. Frame it as ROI: "Our average member increases annual origination income by $X within Y months."
Add 1-2 detailed case studies with before/after metrics, timeline, and the specific GO! strategies that drove results.
Introduce real urgency: next cohort start date, limited coaching spots, or enrollment deadline.
IF we add a pricing/ROI FAQ and a detailed before/after case study
THEN strategy call booking rate will increase for mid-funnel visitors
BECAUSE addressing the price objection proactively reduces anxiety, and case studies provide the evidence needed for System 2 decision-making in high-ticket purchases

5 Mobile Experience

▾

What's Working

Webflow-built site provides a reasonable responsive foundation.
Mobile navigation with hamburger menu is present.

Issues Found

The long hero headline will wrap to 4-5 lines on mobile, pushing the CTA well below the fold. Mobile visitors won't see the action step without scrolling.
Shopping cart flyout with placeholder items may overlay critical content on mobile screens.
Multiple scrolling logo bars and marquee text animations may cause performance issues and layout shifts on lower-end mobile devices.
If CTAs are broken on desktop, they're broken on mobile too — and mobile visitors are even less tolerant of dead-end taps.

Recommendations

Ensure the primary CTA is visible within the first mobile viewport (above the fold on a 390px-wide screen).
Add a sticky mobile CTA bar at the bottom of the screen that persists on scroll — "Book a Free Call" always accessible.
Test page load speed on mobile (target <3 seconds) and reduce animation complexity if needed.
IF we add a sticky mobile CTA bar and shorten the hero headline for mobile
THEN mobile conversion rate will increase
BECAUSE persistent CTAs eliminate the need to scroll back to take action, and shorter headlines ensure the CTA is visible in the first mobile viewport

Methodology

This audit was performed using established CRO frameworks and heuristic analysis. No analytics data was available; all findings are expert-opinion and framework-based.

Conversion Equation

Motivation − Friction − Anxiety + Incentive = Conversion Likelihood. Applied to evaluate each page section's contribution to or detraction from conversion.

Visitor 5 Questions

Every page must answer: Am I in the right place? Is this for me? Can I trust you? What will it cost? What do I do next?

ICE Scoring

Impact × Confidence × Ease, each 1-10. Confidence capped at 6 (heuristic ceiling) due to absence of behavioral analytics data.

Decision Design

Design for System 1 (fast, intuitive) first. System 2 (analytical) only where the purchase decision requires it — applicable for high-ticket coaching.

Data Sources

Homepage content fetched and analyzed on February 13, 2026. Search results used to review program pages, team page, events page, and competitor positioning. No GA4, heatmap, or session recording data was available.
