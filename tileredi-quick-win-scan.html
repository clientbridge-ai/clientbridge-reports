01 — Snapshot
What We Found

Tile Redi has a mature, well-structured Shopify site with a strong product taxonomy, a clear PRO channel separation, and a recently expanded catalog (Redi Door™ shower doors, Surfaces tiles, flat bottom pans). The site's core SEO foundations are largely solid. The highest-impact gaps are concentrated in three areas: title tag and H1 optimization on collection pages, missing mid-funnel content for high-intent comparison keywords, and underutilized structured data for product and FAQ schema. These are fixable within 30 days.

02 — Priority Actions
5 Highest-Impact Quick Wins
#1 — Rewrite Collection Page Title Tags & H1s
High Impact Easy

Key collection pages — including /collections/shower-pans-and-bases, /collections/redi-trench-shower-pans-and-bases, and /collections/barrier-free — use generic or brand-centric title tags rather than keyword-rich, intent-matched titles. For example, the main shower pans collection currently surfaces with a meta title that leads with the brand name rather than the primary search query. Competitors like Laticrete (HYDRO BAN) and wedi are increasingly competitive on "tileable shower pan" and "ready-to-tile shower base" terms.

Action Rewrite title tags on the top 8–10 collection pages following the pattern: [Primary Keyword] | [Secondary Descriptor] — Tile Redi. Example: "Tileable Shower Pans & Bases — One-Piece, Leakproof | Tile Redi". Ensure H1 on each page matches the primary keyword, not just the brand product name.
⏱ Timeline: Week 1 — 3–4 hours of dev/content work
#2 — Add Product Schema Markup to Top-Selling SKUs
High Impact Medium Effort

Product pages lack structured data (Schema.org Product markup with price, availability, and review aggregates). For a Shopify store with hundreds of SKUs in a purchase-intent category, rich snippets (star ratings, price) in SERPs directly improve click-through rates — typically 15–30% CTR lift on product searches. The homepage and FAQ content also lack FAQ schema, which is a straightforward win given the robust FAQ section already present at /pages/technical-information-faq.

Action Enable Shopify's built-in structured data or install a schema app (e.g., Schema Plus or JSON-LD for SEO). Prioritize: (1) Product schema on top 20 SKUs, (2) FAQ schema on the FAQ page, (3) BreadcrumbList schema sitewide. All can be implemented via a Shopify app with no dev work.
⏱ Timeline: Week 1–2 — App install + configuration, ~4 hours
#3 — Create a "Tile Redi vs. [Competitor]" or "Shower Pan Buying Guide" Content Asset
High Impact Medium Effort

A significant share of mid-funnel search traffic for this category happens on comparison and buying guide queries: "tileable shower pan vs acrylic," "best shower pan for tile," "wedi vs tile redi," "how to choose a shower pan." Third-party sites (Innovate Building Solutions, TileThis.com) are capturing this traffic because Tile Redi's own blog and content hub doesn't have a comprehensive evergreen buyer's guide that competes for these terms. The blog (/blogs/blog) has content but no high-authority pillar piece targeting these comparison queries.

Action Publish a 1,500–2,000 word "Complete Shower Pan Buying Guide" targeting queries like "best tileable shower pan," "shower pan for tile installation," and "shower base comparison." Include: material types, drain configurations, curbed vs. curbless, installation complexity, and why one-piece construction matters. Link from homepage and collection pages. This page can capture informational traffic and convert it to product pages.
⏱ Timeline: Week 2–3 — 1 day of content creation
#4 — Optimize Image Alt Text Across Product & Collection Pages
Medium Impact Easy

The site uses heavily templated image filenames and alt text patterns (e.g., manilapan_entrance_rbold_mdrec_sc_cir_c_pc-min). This provides zero semantic signal to Google's image indexer and misses an opportunity for image search traffic — especially meaningful in a visually-driven remodeling category where customers frequently search Google Images for shower pan styles and bathroom inspiration.

Action Audit and rewrite alt text for hero images on top 30 collection and product pages. Pattern: "[Product Name] — [Key Feature], [Color/Finish]". Example: "Redi Trench Linear Drain Shower Pan 60x32 — Brushed Nickel Grate." Shopify allows bulk alt text editing via the Files section or a third-party app like Image Optimizer.
⏱ Timeline: Week 2 — 3–5 hours of bulk editing
#5 — Add Internal Linking From Blog & Knowledge Center to Commercial Pages
Medium Impact Easy

The Knowledge Center and blog sections (/blogs/blog, /pages/technical-information-installation, /pages/technical-information-faq) contain valuable content but have limited internal links pointing to commercial collection pages. This means any authority those informational pages accumulate is not flowing to the revenue-generating product and collection pages. This is especially visible on installation guide pages, which naturally attract contractor and DIY audiences who are purchase-ready.

Action Conduct a content audit of the top 15 Knowledge Center and blog pages. Add 2–3 contextual internal links per page pointing to the most relevant collection or product pages. Prioritize: installation guides → shower pan collections; FAQ pages → product pages; blog posts → new tile collection. Use descriptive anchor text (e.g., "one-piece tileable shower pan" not "click here").
⏱ Timeline: Week 3 — 4–6 hours of content editing

04 — Site Structure
Structural Observations

✓
Strong Product Taxonomy

The navigation and collection structure is well-organized. Separate collections for each product line (Redi Base, Redi Trench, Corner, Barrier Free, etc.) create crawlable, indexable facets that Google can parse independently. This is a significant advantage over competitors with flat catalogs.

✓
PRO Channel Separation

The commercial.tileredi.com subdomain for contractor/PRO pricing is a smart SEO move — it avoids keyword cannibalization between DIY and trade audiences, and allows the main domain to focus signals on consumer purchase intent queries.

⚠
Duplicate Footer Navigation

The site renders navigation links twice in the DOM (desktop and mobile menus both present in HTML). This is standard for Shopify themes but can dilute internal link equity by splitting link signals across duplicate anchor elements. Confirm canonical handling is correct.

⚠
Thin Collection Page Copy

Several collection pages (shower doors, tiles) have minimal descriptive text below the fold. Google uses this copy to understand page relevance. Adding 150–250 words of keyword-rich, buyer-focused copy to each main collection page would meaningfully improve topical relevance signals.

✗
Missing Schema Markup

No Product schema, FAQ schema, or BreadcrumbList schema detected on reviewed pages. For an eCommerce site with hundreds of products in a purchase-intent category, this is the single highest-leverage technical fix available. Rich results directly improve SERP click-through rates.

→
New Product Lines Need SEO Ramp

Shower Doors (Redi Door™), Tiles (Surfaces by Tile Redi™), and Flat Bottom Shower Pans are new additions. These collection pages will need time and deliberate internal linking / content support to build ranking authority. Start building internal links to these pages now to accelerate indexation and authority accumulation.
