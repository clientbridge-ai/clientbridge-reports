Reputation & Reviews
Priority #2 (tied with CRO) · The trust layer that makes everything else work
$0
Add'l spend req'd

Velocity & Generation

Oakland Ave is below the 4.0 threshold — 3.9★ materially suppresses local pack visibility. Google's algorithm treats sub-4.0 as a negative ranking signal. Getting to 4.1+ is a higher priority than any content or link-building work for that location.
Build a post-visit review request sequence in Mindbody — automated SMS or email sent 2–4 hours after appointment. Target: 15+ new reviews/month across both locations combined. This single tactic has more local pack impact than most SEO work.
Train front desk on verbal review requests — a simple "If you loved your experience, a Google review means the world to us" at checkout converts at 20–30%. No app or software required.
Diversify beyond Google — for med spas, RealSelf is the highest-authority review platform for injectables and aesthetics. Yelp and Healthgrades also rank in local search and influence first-time buyers researching providers before they ever reach the website.
Tie review milestones to team recognition — review velocity is a team sport. Locations that hit monthly targets get called out. Staff who generate the most review mentions by name get recognized. Culture drives volume.

Response & Recovery

Respond to every review within 48 hours — Google's algorithm rewards review response rate and recency. Potential clients read owner responses before booking. A thoughtful response to a negative review often converts skeptics into clients.
Establish a negative review protocol — never argue publicly. Template: "We're sorry your experience didn't meet our standards — please reach out to [name] at [email] so we can make it right." HIPAA note: never confirm or reference treatment details in any public response.
Audit existing negative reviews for operational signals — recurring themes in 1–2 star reviews (wait times, specific staff, specific services) are operational problems, not just reputation problems. Fix the root cause, then drive new positive volume to dilute the negatives.
Deploy reviews as a marketing asset — Google Search Ads support seller ratings extensions (auto-populated from review platforms). Pull standout quotes for the website homepage near the Book Now CTA. The "Voted #1 Med Spa in Austin" claim needs visible review volume behind it to remain credible.
Track competitor review velocity monthly — monitor Viva Day Spa and Milk and Honey review counts. Reviews compound over time: the location with the most recent, highest-volume reviews wins the local pack algorithm. If competitors are accelerating, LVS must match pace or the gap widens permanently.

Reactivation Campaigns
Highest immediate ROI — start here
Segment lapsed clients immediately — pull all Mindbody contacts with no booking in 90+ days. This is the reactivation list. It's likely 60–70% of the database and represents the highest-value first campaign.
Send a "We miss you" reactivation SMS — SMS converts 3–5× better than email for appointment-based businesses. A simple offer (10–15% off their next service, or a complimentary add-on) with a direct booking link. Keep it personal: "Hi [first name], it's been a while — we'd love to see you back."
Follow up with email 3 days later — for contacts who didn't respond to SMS. Slightly longer format: remind them what services they've had before, highlight anything new (new service, new staff, new location hours), include a clear Book Now button.
Segment by service history for relevance — massage clients get a massage offer. Botox clients get an injectable touch-up reminder. Facial clients get a seasonal skin care message. Generic "come back" messages underperform targeted ones by 40–60%.
Run reactivation quarterly — anyone who hasn't booked in 90 days gets a touchpoint. Set this up once in Mindbody as an automated flow; it runs itself.
🔄
Retention & Lifecycle Sequences
Turn one-time buyers into regulars
Pre-book at checkout — the single highest-leverage retention tactic. "Would you like to schedule your next appointment before you leave?" A client who leaves with a future booking has a 70%+ retention rate vs. 30% who don't. No software needed — it's a front desk habit.
Service-specific rebooking reminders — Botox wears off in 3–4 months; fillers in 6–12. Set automated Mindbody reminders timed to each service: "Your Botox results typically start to fade around now — ready to refresh?" This alone could double injectable rebooking rates.
Cross-sell sequences — a client who only gets massages has never been introduced to Botox or facials. A client who gets Botox has never tried cryotherapy. Build simple email sequences that introduce complementary services 30 days after a first booking in any category.
Birthday offer — automated SMS/email 1 week before a client's birthday with a small offer (complimentary add-on, priority booking, or modest discount). Mindbody has birthdate data. This campaign runs at near-zero cost and generates outsized goodwill.
Promote membership/package pricing — recurring revenue is the most valuable revenue. If LVS has any membership or package structure in Mindbody, the ~4,000-contact database is the audience for it. A monthly massage membership converts best to clients who've visited 2+ times.

Seasonal Campaign Calendar
12 months of reasons to book
January — "New Year, New You" skin reset / Botox refresh campaign. Highest-intent month for aesthetic services after holiday indulgence.
February — Valentine's Day couples massage packages. Gift card push. Start campaign 2 weeks early — buyers decide fast for this holiday.
April/May — Mother's Day gift cards and packages. Historically the #1 gift card sales period for spa businesses. Run SMS campaign to all clients 3 weeks out.
June — Summer body prep (cryotherapy, body contouring, IV hydration). Austin summer is brutal — lean into wellness and recovery messaging.
October/November — Holiday event prep (Botox, fillers, facials). "Look your best for the holidays" campaigns convert strongly 6–8 weeks before Thanksgiving.
Referral campaign (ongoing) — ~4,000 happy customers who've never been asked to refer. A simple "Give $25, Get $25" referral SMS to the full list is a low-cost acquisition campaign with built-in trust transfer. Run it quarterly.
⚙️
Mindbody Setup & Execution
The tool is already paid for — use it
Audit Mindbody contact data quality first — before sending anything, verify: what % of the ~4,000 contacts have valid mobile numbers? Valid emails? Are contacts tagged by service history and location? Bad data = wasted sends and deliverability damage.
Confirm SMS opt-in compliance — TCPA requires explicit opt-in for marketing SMS. Mindbody captures this at booking, but verify opt-in status before the first blast. Sending to non-opted-in contacts creates legal exposure.
Mindbody has email and SMS built in — no additional software required to start. Campaigns, automated flows, and segmentation are all available in the existing subscription. The cost to execute this entire channel is essentially $0 beyond staff time.
If Mindbody email/SMS is limited — consider integrating with Klaviyo (email) or a dedicated SMS tool like Podium or Birdeye. These also double as review request platforms, consolidating the reputation and retention stack.
Track CRM revenue separately — tag all bookings that originate from email or SMS campaigns in Mindbody. This makes the ROI of the retention channel visible and builds the case for continued investment in the database.
