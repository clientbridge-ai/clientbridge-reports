Detailed Findings

1. Message Match & Clarity

5 / 10 ▼

What's Working

  • The hero headline "Get Hired Faster." is concise and outcome-oriented
  • Sub-navigation clearly segments audiences: Job Seekers, Fractional, Boards, Businesses

Issues Found

  • Hero headline is too generic. "Get Hired Faster" could apply to a $15/hr job board or a $15K executive coaching program. It doesn't signal executive-level positioning, price point, or differentiation.
  • No audience qualifier above the fold. A VP-level executive landing on this page must scroll to "Is Your Job Search Stuck?" to confirm this is for them. By then, many have bounced.
  • Multiple competing CTAs in the hero. "What We Offer," "Success Stories," and "Client Reviews" all compete for attention. There's no single clear next step.
  • "Is Your Job Search Stuck?" section uses a pain-point checklist (too old, too specialized, no degree) that risks making high-status executives feel talked down to. The framing is "liabilities" rather than "strategic advantages waiting to be unlocked."

Recommendations

  • Replace hero headline with audience-specific, outcome-quantified copy. Example: "Executive-Level Career Strategy for Leaders Targeting $250K+ Roles" — immediately signals who, what, and tier.
  • Add a qualifying subhead: "For VPs, SVPs, and C-Suite leaders in career transition" directly beneath the headline.
  • Reduce hero to a single primary CTA: "Schedule Your Executive Consult."
  • Reframe the "stuck" section with empowering language: "Where You Are Now → Where We Take You."
IF we replace "Get Hired Faster" with an audience-qualified, outcome-specific headline and reduce to one primary CTA
THEN homepage-to-contact conversion rate will increase for executive-level visitors
BECAUSE specificity answers "Am I in the right place?" within 3 seconds (Message Match principle) and reduces decision paralysis from competing CTAs

2. Value Proposition

5 / 10 ▼

What's Working

  • The services page has a strong four-stage process framework (Positioning → Build → Visibility → Conversion)
  • "40 years" of experience is cited — longevity is a powerful trust differentiator
  • JobRAMP proprietary tool creates tangible differentiation vs. generic career coaches

Issues Found

  • Homepage "How We Can Help" is feature-focused, not outcome-focused. "Strategic Support," "Personal Website," "Resumes & More" describe deliverables, not results. Executives buy outcomes (faster hires, higher comp), not features.
  • No quantified results on the homepage. "Decades of Expertise" and "Proven Results" are claims without numbers. How many placements? Average time-to-hire? Salary increase?
  • "Why us vs. alternatives?" is never answered. There's no competitor differentiation. An executive comparing ECP to Korn Ferry outplacement, The Muse, or a solo coach has no framework for deciding.
  • Pricing signals are absent. For a high-ticket service, the complete absence of pricing (no ranges, no "starting at," no "packages from") creates anxiety and filters out serious buyers who want to self-qualify.

Recommendations

  • Add a "By the Numbers" banner to the homepage: placements made, average time-to-placement, average salary increase, client satisfaction rate.
  • Rewrite the 6 service cards as outcome statements: "Strategic Support" → "Land 3–5x More Interviews in 60 Days."
  • Add a comparison section or FAQ entry: "How ECP compares to outplacement firms, recruiters, and solo coaches."
  • Add pricing signals: "Our programs typically range from $X–$Y depending on scope" or "Investment starts at $X."
IF we add quantified client outcomes and pricing signals to the homepage
THEN contact form submissions will increase and lead quality will improve
BECAUSE specificity increases motivation (Conversion Equation) and pricing transparency reduces anxiety while pre-qualifying buyers

3. Trust & Credibility

6 / 10 ▼

What's Working

  • Client success stories page is excellent — named clients with photos, titles, companies, locations, and LinkedIn links. This is high-quality social proof.
  • Testimonial carousel on homepage provides at-a-glance credibility
  • Physical address and phone number in footer and contact page
  • Video testimonials are referenced (Vimeo embeds)

Issues Found

  • Testimonials on homepage are generic. "Outstanding and beneficial experience" and "can best be described as exceptional" don't convey specific outcomes. Compare to: "Landed a COO role in 47 days with a $40K salary increase."
  • No Google Reviews or third-party validation visible. For a high-ticket service, linking to Google, Trustpilot, or BBB reviews adds independent credibility that self-hosted testimonials can't.
  • Success stories are buried 2+ clicks deep. The most powerful conversion asset on the site requires navigation to find. None of the detailed stories appear on the homepage.
  • No team credentials above the fold. Visitors don't know who will be working with them. Adding a strategist's photo and bio near the CTA would humanize the service.

Recommendations

  • Replace homepage testimonial quotes with outcome-specific versions: include job title landed, timeline, and one quantified result.
  • Add a Google Reviews widget or badge linking to verified third-party reviews.
  • Surface 2–3 mini case studies on the homepage (name, title, industry pivot, days-to-hire).
  • Add a "Your Strategist" preview section near the primary CTA with photo, name, and credentials.
IF we replace generic testimonials with outcome-specific proof and surface case studies on the homepage
THEN time-on-page will increase and contact rate will improve
BECAUSE specific social proof reduces anxiety at the decision point (Cialdini's social proof principle) and keeps visitors from needing to hunt for validation

4. CTA & Conversion Path

4 / 10 ▼

What's Working

  • "Start Now" button in the nav provides persistent access to contact
  • Services page ends with clear "Contact Us" CTA

Issues Found

  • Homepage has 7+ competing CTAs in the hero section. "What We Offer," "Success Stories," "Client Reviews," plus social media links and a video — all above the fold. This violates the one-primary-CTA-per-viewport rule.
  • "Start Now" is vague. Start what? The CTA copy doesn't communicate what happens next. "Schedule a Free Consult" or "Get Your Career Strategy" is more specific and less risky-sounding.
  • No CTA after the testimonial section. The homepage testimonial carousel — a high-motivation moment — is followed by blog posts instead of a conversion opportunity.
  • The "Get Help Now" button links to a liabilities page, not a contact form. This is a conversion path detour that adds an unnecessary step.
  • No secondary CTA for lower-intent visitors. Someone not ready to "Start Now" has no option to download a guide, take an assessment, or watch a webinar.

Recommendations

  • Reduce homepage hero to one primary CTA: "Schedule Your Free Executive Consult."
  • Rename "Start Now" to "Free Strategy Call" or "Book a Consult" throughout the site.
  • Add a CTA block immediately after the testimonial carousel: "Ready to start your career transition? [Book Your Consult]."
  • Add a secondary lead magnet CTA: "Download: The Executive Job Search Playbook" — captures leads who aren't ready for a call.
  • Fix the "Get Help Now" link to go directly to contact, or inline the assessment on the contact page.
IF we reduce to one primary CTA per viewport and add a post-testimonial conversion block
THEN click-through rate to the contact page will increase significantly
BECAUSE decision paralysis from competing CTAs is the #1 conversion killer (Hick's Law), and placing CTAs at peak-motivation moments captures intent

5. Form / Checkout UX

4 / 10 ▼

What's Working

  • Contact page includes a video to set expectations before the form
  • Resume upload is marked optional, reducing friction

Issues Found

  • Contact form requires JavaScript to render. The page shows "JavaScript is required for this content" — the form itself isn't visible in a standard fetch. If JS fails to load or loads slowly, the visitor sees no form at all.
  • No indication of what happens after form submission. Will they get a call? An email? Within what timeframe? Uncertainty at the final conversion step causes abandonment.
  • Contact page headline "Contact Us" is generic. This is the final conversion page — it should reinforce the value proposition: "Let's Build Your Executive Career Strategy — Schedule a Free 30-Minute Consult."
  • No trust reinforcements on the contact page. No testimonial snippets, no "500+ executives served," no privacy assurance near the form.
  • Phone number is present but not positioned as an alternative conversion path. Some executives prefer to call. A "Prefer to talk? Call (303) 529-4470" placed prominently near the form would capture this segment.

Recommendations

  • Add a fallback static form or noscript message so the conversion path never breaks.
  • Add "What Happens Next" copy below the form: "Within 24 hours, you'll receive a personal email from a Career Strategist to schedule your complimentary consult."
  • Rewrite the contact page headline to reinforce value and set expectations.
  • Add 1–2 micro-testimonials beside the form: short quotes with client photos.
  • Prominently feature the phone number as an alternative CTA beside the form.
IF we add "What Happens Next" copy and trust signals adjacent to the contact form
THEN form completion rate will increase
BECAUSE reducing uncertainty at the final conversion step directly addresses the "What will it cost or risk?" visitor question and lowers anxiety

6. Content & Persuasion

6 / 10 ▼

What's Working

  • Services page uses a comprehensive FAQ section that addresses real objections (confidential searches, industry pivots, layoff stigma)
  • Blog content is current (Feb 2026) and topically relevant — layoff context creates urgency
  • Success stories provide narrative proof with real career pivots (retail → COO, airline → AWS, sales → HR)
  • The four-stage process reduces perceived complexity of the engagement

Issues Found

  • No urgency or loss framing anywhere on the site. For unemployed executives, time is money — every week without a role costs real income. The site doesn't frame inaction as costly.
  • The mission statement is used as a content block. "Our mission is to bring strength and dignity to professionals..." appears on both the homepage and services page. Missions are internal documents, not conversion copy.
  • Success stories lack outcome metrics. We learn that clients got hired, but not: how fast, at what salary level, or with what comp increase. "Landed in 47 days" is more persuasive than "found a job."
  • Homepage blog section adds length without conversion value. Three blog previews push the footer CTA further down the page with content that serves SEO, not conversion.

Recommendations

  • Add a cost-of-inaction calculation: "Every month without your next executive role costs you $X in lost compensation. Most ECP clients are in their new role within 60–90 days."
  • Replace the mission statement with a client-facing value headline.
  • Add outcome metrics to at least 3 success stories: days to placement, salary level, and industry.
  • Move or remove blog previews from the homepage; replace with a CTA block or additional social proof.
IF we add loss framing and quantified timelines to the homepage
THEN urgency and motivation will increase, driving more consult bookings
BECAUSE loss aversion is 2x more motivating than equivalent gains (Kahneman), and time-specificity makes the outcome feel achievable

7. Mobile Experience

6 / 10 ▼

What's Working

  • Site uses responsive WordPress theme — layouts adapt to smaller screens
  • Click-to-call phone link is present in the footer
  • Navigation appears to collapse into a mobile hamburger menu

Issues Found

  • No sticky mobile CTA. On a long-scrolling homepage, the "Start Now" button disappears once a user scrolls past the header. A persistent bottom-bar CTA would maintain the conversion path.
  • Hero section with multiple CTA buttons likely stacks poorly on mobile. Three side-by-side buttons create a confusing tap-target cluster on small screens.
  • Video autoplay/embed on contact page may slow mobile load. The Vimeo embed adds weight to the most critical conversion page.
  • Contact form JS dependency is a mobile risk. Slower connections and ad blockers on mobile increase the chance of the form failing to render.

Recommendations

  • Add a sticky mobile CTA bar at the bottom of the screen: "Book Your Consult" + phone icon.
  • Simplify mobile hero to a single CTA button with adequate tap-target size (min 44px).
  • Lazy-load the Vimeo embed on the contact page; show a thumbnail with play button.
  • Add a static HTML fallback form for cases where JS doesn't load.
IF we add a sticky mobile CTA bar
THEN mobile contact page visits will increase
BECAUSE persistent access to the conversion path reduces friction on long mobile pages where navigation disappears on scroll

Methodology

This audit was conducted using heuristic CRO analysis against established conversion optimization frameworks. No analytics data was available; all confidence scores are capped at 6.

Frameworks Applied

  • Conversion Equation: Motivation − Friction − Anxiety + Incentive = Conversion Likelihood
  • Visitor 5 Questions: Am I in the right place? Is this for me? Can I trust you? What will it cost? What do I do next?
  • Decision Design: System 1 (fast/intuitive) before System 2 (analytical)
  • ICE Prioritization: Impact × Confidence × Ease scoring for experiment sequencing
  • Hick's Law: Reducing choices reduces decision time and increases conversion
  • Cialdini Principles: Social proof, authority, scarcity, loss aversion
