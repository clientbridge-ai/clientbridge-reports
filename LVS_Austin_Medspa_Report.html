01 Executive Summary

LVS La Vie Med Spas operates two Austin locations with a loyal core following built around owner Anna Vickmay's personal expertise and a warm, boutique atmosphere. The brand has real strengths — authentic clinical skill, a distinctive Rainey Street location, and a full-stack service menu that few competitors can match.


However, it faces serious structural vulnerabilities: a thin online review footprint relative to dominant players, a brand identity crisis created by a confusing name change (Vickmay → La Vie En Rainey → LVS La Vie Med Spas), and operational failures around pricing transparency and scheduling that are actively costing it business.


Meanwhile, competitors Viva and milk + honey are deeply entrenched with institutional brand recognition, and Rejuvenate Austin owns the physician-led clinical credibility lane. LVS needs to stop competing on all fronts and pick a lane — fast.

The U.S. medical spa market is one of the fastest-growing segments in all of wellness. The adult 25–55 segment accounts for the majority of revenue, with injectables and facial treatments leading service volume. Body contouring and sculpting are the fastest-growing category.

Austin is particularly well-positioned: a tech-economy demographic with high disposable incomes, an established culture of personal wellness, and a young professional base (ages 25–38) that skews heavily toward the Rainey Street neighborhood — LVS's front door.

Key Trends Shaping the Austin Market

  • Body contouring / sculpting is the fastest-growing service category — LVS offers this but doesn't lead with it in messaging
  • "Baby Botox" is driving 25–34 adoption — Austin's Rainey Street demographic is squarely in this segment; LVS's location is an asset hiding in plain sight
  • Wellness integration is now expected — IV therapy, weight management, and peptides alongside traditional aesthetics. LVS has this full stack; most competitors don't
  • Male clientele is growing — LVS has a Men's Spa section but minimal dedicated messaging or activation around it
  • Austin's Southern U.S. position + tech-economy demographics = above-average spending capacity and aesthetic awareness
  • Consolidation is accelerating — PE-backed multi-location brands are raising the brand consistency bar. Independent medspas need a sharper identity to compete

03 LVS La Vie — Brand Audit

Website & Positioning

The lvmedspas.com site positions LVS as a full-service luxury wellness destination — a woman-owned business where advanced aesthetics and luxury self-care converge. The service menu is genuinely impressive: injectables, laser, chemical peels, body sculpting, IV therapy, peptides, salon, cryotherapy, weight loss (semaglutide), and sexual wellness. Very few competitors offer this breadth.

The site's seasonal promotions (SXSW 2026, Spring Glow Refresh) and the interactive skin self-assessment CTA both show marketing creativity. However, the homepage headline — "Experience Radiance in Austin" — is completely generic, indistinguishable from a dozen competitors.

Working
  • Broadest service menu in Austin market — injectable + IV + sculpting + peptides + semaglutide + laser + salon + cryo under one roof
  • Owner Anna Vickmay's personal expertise drives fierce loyalty — "voted #1 Med Spa in Austin 5 years"
  • Champagne + hospitality experience is memorable and brand-defining
  • Rainey Street location is a destination — foot traffic, atmosphere, and lifestyle alignment competitors can't replicate
  • SXSW 2026 activation page shows real marketing creativity
  • Natural-looking results repeatedly praised — "true artists at their craft"
  • Personalized skin self-assessment CTA is smart and differentiating
  • Men's Spa section represents an underactivated but real opportunity
Needs Fixing
  • Brand name confusion: Vickmay → La Vie En Rainey → LVS La Vie — Instagram, website, and Google all say different things, fragmenting search authority
  • Only 78–86 Google reviews total — Viva has 600+ at ONE location; this suppresses local search ranking and first-impression trust
  • ~20% one-star rate on Google — pricing surprises, unexpected closures, salon quality failures
  • Salon services generating worst reviews, diluting med spa brand equity
  • No prominent physician/medical credential featured — a major gap vs. Rejuvenate and Viva
  • Instagram: only 1,969 followers — critically underdeveloped for a brand claiming 5x "Best Med Spa"
  • Homepage headline is completely generic — "Experience Radiance in Austin" could be anyone
  • No publicly visible pricing — feeding the pricing surprise complaints

Negative Themes — Where LVS Is Losing Clients

Pricing discrepancies and transparency failures
Discrepancies between quoted and charged amounts, and "unprofessional behavior regarding treatment costs." This is the #1 trust-killer and one of the easiest things to fix — yet it keeps recurring.
Unexpected closures during business hours
Clients arriving during listed hours to find the location closed. A brand-damaging reliability failure, especially for a business built on trust and appointments.
Salon quality failures
Detailed, credible accounts of hair color disasters: wrong results, dye left on ears and forehead, incorrect professional advice ("it'll lighten with washing" — factually false for permanent color). The salon side is generating the majority of the 1-star reviews.
Scheduling and communication failures
Difficulty booking, confusion around appointment confirmations, inconsistent follow-through. For a luxury brand, friction here is unacceptable.
Location findability
The embedded nature of the Rainey Street location creates a friction point for first-time clients. At least one reviewer noted difficulty finding it even after arriving in the area.

05 Competitor Analysis

milk + honey

Award-winning luxury spa + medspa. Founded 2006. Multi-city.
Strengths
  • Austin brand institution since 2006 — deeply entrenched
  • Design-forward spaces in premium locations (hotel, Arboretum)
  • Gift card market dominance — "Austin's favorite gift"
  • Multi-city expansion (Houston, Fort Worth, Chicago, LA) signals national credibility
  • Full service stack: spa + salon + medspa (Botox, fillers, BBL/Moxi, microneedling)
  • Consistently voted Best Spa by Austin Chronicle readers
Vulnerabilities
  • Premium pricing over-delivers on setting, under-delivers on clinical results
  • Last-minute appointment cancellations are a recurring complaint — charges clients for lateness but cancels with no penalty
  • No IV therapy, no weight management, no wellness stack
  • High internal staff turnover flagged on Indeed and Glassdoor — will eventually surface in service quality
  • Feeling more corporate as it scales nationally

Rejuvenate Austin

Physician-owned boutique medspa. Dr. Jessica Wright. Single location.
Strengths
  • Physician credibility is unmatched — Dr. Wright performs all injectables and Ultherapy personally
  • Named "The Face of Anti-aging and Rejuvenation" by Austin Monthly
  • CoolSculpting University certified — leads in body contouring credibility
  • #1 on Yelp's Austin Medical Spa ranking
  • Strong personal brand on Instagram; Dr. Wright is the face
  • Boutique, high-trust feel — clients feel genuinely cared for, not processed
Vulnerabilities
  • Closed weekends — a structural friction point for working professionals
  • Single location limits reach and availability
  • No spa services, no IV, no wellness, no salon — nothing for the whole-body client
  • Limited service breadth means one-trick pony risk as competitors expand
  • Some clients report not achieving desired results and needing follow-up

Viva Day Spa

Austin's dominant multi-location spa institution. Founded 2005. 4 ATX locations + Dallas.
Strengths
  • 4 Austin locations + Dallas — broadest reach of any competitor
  • Voted Best Spa in Austin repeatedly by Austin Chronicle and Austin Monthly
  • 600+ reviews at a single location — local SEO dominance
  • Board-certified plastic surgeon (Dr. Aisha White) at Domain location
  • 9am–9pm daily, 7 days a week — best accessibility in the market
  • Strong couples and prenatal positioning; covers the broadest demographic
Vulnerabilities
  • Feels institutional and impersonal at scale — "Stepford Wives" model noted internally
  • High employee turnover, toxic culture pockets flagged on Glassdoor
  • No meaningful wellness/IV differentiation — limited beyond traditional spa + injectables
  • "Best Spa" positioning is legacy — hasn't launched meaningfully new concepts in years
  • Some scheduling issues and pricing miscommunications at scale

07 Messaging Gaps & Whitespace

What All Competitors Are Saying (Avoid / Transcend)

  • "Luxury experience" — every competitor uses this exact language; it means nothing
  • "Results-driven" — completely commoditized at this point
  • "Personalized treatments" — generic category claim; zero differentiation
  • "Award-winning" — milk + honey and Viva own this; LVS self-claiming it falls flat without supporting evidence
  • "Feel confident and radiant" — the category cliché every medspa reaches for

What Customers Want That Nobody Is Marketing

  • A provider who's "not pushy" — mentioned repeatedly in LVS reviews and a real pain point across the industry. Nobody is building a brand around this explicitly, yet it's a top client desire.
  • "Natural-looking results" — clients are afraid of looking overdone. Several reviews specifically praised LVS for this. Zero competitors are building their brand around the anti-overdone lane.
  • A trusted multi-year relationship — "I've been seeing her for 2 years and am never disappointed." The emotional payoff of finding a provider you trust deeply is the most powerful loyalty driver in the category — and nobody is marketing to it.
  • The full-body transformation journey — clients who want aesthetics AND IV therapy AND weight loss AND sculpting are not being targeted as a cohesive segment anywhere in the Austin market. LVS has the services for this client; no one is speaking directly to them.
  • The champagne / social ritual — LVS's own differentiator that it buries in reviews but doesn't market. The idea of a luxurious, celebratory experience (not just a clinical appointment) is memorable and underutilized.
The Open Lane

Nobody owns: "The results-first wellness boutique for the young Austin professional." Rejuvenate is closed weekends. Viva feels corporate. Milk + honey is expensive and light on clinical depth. LVS is the only player with a full-stack menu (injectables + IV + body sculpting + weight loss + laser) in a boutique setting near downtown — but it isn't communicating this with clarity or consistency.

LVS's Most Defensible Differentiation Opportunity

The only downtown Austin med spa with a full wellness + medical aesthetics stack, operated by an owner-practitioner with a deeply personal clinical style and a track record of natural, artistic injectable results — in a destination neighborhood that attracts Austin's most aspirational consumers.

Own the anti-overdone lane explicitly. Become the injector brand for clients who are afraid of looking fake — a real, unmet fear in this market. Zero competitors are here.
"Austin's most complete wellness experience."
Lead with the breadth. No competitor combines IV + injectables + body sculpting + weight loss in a Rainey Street boutique. Let the address do the status work.
"The boutique on Rainey Street that changes how you feel about medspas."

"Your face is not a template."

"Results you'll live in, not just leave with."
Targets the whole-body wellness client who wants aesthetic outcomes AND body/health transformation through IV, semaglutide, and sculpting — a cohesive client segment no one is targeting explicitly.
08 Tough Love — Top 3 Brand Vulnerabilities
01
The review gap is existential
With 78–86 total Google reviews across locations and a ~20% one-star rate, LVS is effectively invisible in local search and untrustworthy to first-time evaluators. Viva has 600+ reviews at a single location. Rejuvenate leads Yelp's Austin med spa ranking. LVS doesn't appear prominently anywhere a new Austin resident searching for a med spa would find them. No amount of brand strategy fixes invisibility. This isn't a marketing problem — it's a survival problem.
02
The brand is one person
If Anna Vickmay is unavailable, sick, or at capacity — LVS has no brand. The business model as currently operated is a high-risk, owner-dependent practice dressed up as a spa brand. For scale, for resilience, or for any future exit, this has to change structurally. A competitor who systemically targeted LVS's clients would simply position around consistency, a clear medical credential, and multi-provider availability — and they would convert a meaningful portion of LVS's base.
03
The salon is actively diluting brand equity
The med spa reviews are consistently strong. The salon reviews are generating the worst complaints — bad hair color, poor technique, incorrect professional advice, dye left on skin. When a potential med spa client reads a 1-star review about a haircut, they don't compartmentalize. They leave. Either dramatically raise the salon standard (hire, train, audit regularly) or stop leading with it in the brand story. It is currently the single biggest source of reputational damage.
09 Top 3 Highest-Leverage Opportunities
01
The "not pushy" positioning is up for grabs
Multiple LVS reviews specifically mention that staff are knowledgeable and "not pushy." In an industry where upselling and aggressive service recommendations are endemic complaints, this is genuinely differentiating — and LVS isn't using it in marketing at all. One campaign built around "No pressure. Just results." or "We'll tell you what you actually need" could convert every competitor's frustrated client. This message is credible, ownable, and unoccupied.
02
The wellness stack is undermonetized and undermarketed
LVS is one of the only Austin medspas offering IV therapy, semaglutide weight loss, peptides, cryotherapy, and sexual wellness alongside traditional aesthetics. This is a meaningful differentiator in 2026 as the market shifts toward holistic body optimization. Rejuvenate doesn't offer it. Milk + honey doesn't offer it. Viva barely touches it. LVS has this full stack and barely mentions it. A "body optimization" or "wellness transformation" positioning campaign that packages these services together could attract a premium, high-LTV client segment that no competitor is targeting.
03
Rainey Street is a destination asset, not just an address
The champagne experience, the atmosphere, the social energy of the neighborhood — this is an experiential differentiator that no other Austin medspa can claim. Yet LVS markets it like a generic spa with a nice address. Leaning into the lifestyle/destination angle — spa parties, events, bachelorette packages, SXSW activations (which they've started), date-night packages — creates word-of-mouth and organic Instagram content that no paid campaign can replicate. The Rainey Street brand association is free advertising that is being almost entirely wasted.
10 First 90 Days — CMO Priority Roadmap
Days 1–30
Triage & Foundation
  • Respond professionally to every 1-star Google review within 7 days — no templates, genuine responses
  • Implement post-service review solicitation via MindBody text/email — make it frictionless
  • Consolidate brand name — pick one (recommend LVS La Vie) and unify across Google Business Profiles, website, Instagram, and all platforms
  • Publish all service pricing publicly on the website; eliminate the #1 complaint driver immediately
  • Fix both Google Business Profiles: photos, hours, categories, Q&A, and accurate location pin
  • Audit salon staffing and establish a minimum quality standard or decision to deprioritize
Days 31–60
Messaging & Content
  • Develop 3 core positioning messages: natural-looking results, full wellness stack, no-pressure consultations
  • Build Instagram content calendar anchored to before/afters (with consent), staff features, and behind-the-scenes Rainey Street lifestyle content
  • Feature 2–3 named providers alongside Anna — begin distributing brand equity beyond one person
  • Goal: 10 posts in 30 days, double engagement rate from baseline
  • Develop the "Rainey Glow" signature package concept for launch in Day 61–90
  • Start collecting client testimonial content — video if possible
Days 61–90
Growth & Differentiation
  • Launch targeted Google Ads on high-intent Austin med spa keywords where LVS is not appearing organically
  • Launch "Rainey Glow" package — injectable consultation + facial + champagne — positioned as giftable and bookmark-worthy
  • Launch client referral program — loyal base is the most untapped marketing asset in the business
  • Evaluate formally separating or upgrading the salon brand — make the decision, don't defer it
  • Begin building a wellness bundle campaign targeting the semaglutide + IV + sculpting client segment
