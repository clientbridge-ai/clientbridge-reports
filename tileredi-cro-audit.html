TileRedi.com CRO Audit

Homepage & core e-commerce experience · tileredi.com

Business Type: E-commerce / DTC Manufacturing
Primary Goal: Product Purchase / Add to Cart
Audience: DIY homeowners + Professional contractors
Analytics: Heuristic (no GA4 data)
Date: March 2026

Executive Summary

TileRedi is a legitimate, 25-year-old manufacturer with a genuinely differentiated product — one-piece, leakproof, tileable shower systems. The product story is strong. The website, however, does not tell it well. Key conversion barriers include: a fragmented dual-audience homepage, CTA language that undersells, trust signals buried below conversion friction, and an active shipping delay notice that is raising anxiety without providing reassurance. Revenue is being left on the table.

🚨
Critical Issue: A sitewide banner announcing "We are currently experiencing shipping delays" is front-and-center on the homepage with zero remediation language (no ETA, no compensation, no urgency reframe). For a high-ticket building product where project timelines are critical, this is likely causing significant abandonment before visitors even see the product.
⚠️
Analytics Gap: No GA4/BigQuery data was available for this audit. All scores reflect heuristic analysis (ICE Confidence max = 6). Connecting GA4 to BigQuery should be the first infrastructure action — device split data, landing page bounce by source, and funnel drop-off data would substantially improve prioritization.

Detailed Findings

Click any section to expand findings, recommendations, and testable hypotheses.

01
Message Match & Clarity
6/10
▾

✅ What's Working

  • The brand name "Tile Redi" and tagline "Waterproof Shower Systems" immediately answer "Am I in the right place?" for category-aware visitors.
  • The mega-nav is well-organized with product imagery — a strong visual orienter for first-time visitors.
  • Blog content addresses relevant purchase-phase questions (labor cost, tariffs, installation) — showing good content strategy intent.

⚠️ Issues Found

  • The homepage headline (hero area) does not contain a clear, benefit-led statement above the fold. Visitors see product promotions before understanding why Tile Redi exists.
  • Three separate audience messages compete simultaneously: DIY homeowners, Professional contractors (PRO site CTA), and tile buyers (NEWTILE15 code). No dominant path is established.
  • The active shipping delay banner is placed at the top of every page — message-first real estate being consumed by negative news rather than brand value.
  • "Shop Now" hero CTAs don't specify what's being shopped — too generic for a complex, multi-product category.

💡 Recommendation & Hypothesis

IF the homepage hero is redesigned with a single outcome-led headline ("Build a Leakproof Shower — Faster and Easier Than You Think") and a clear audience fork (DIY vs. PRO) presented as two equal pathways, THEN scroll depth and time-on-site will increase for new visitors, BECAUSE visitors with ambiguous intent will self-select a lane rather than bouncing from an undifferentiated feed.
02
Value Proposition
4/10
▾

✅ What's Working

  • The product has a genuinely strong differentiator: the only manufacturer offering a one-piece tileable shower pan in any size with any drain location. This exists in the About page copy but not the homepage.
  • UL certification is mentioned — a powerful trust signal that builds perceived quality.
  • Bundled pricing ("everything you need right in the box") addresses a real purchasing pain point in this category.

⚠️ Issues Found

  • The homepage hero does not surface the core value proposition. A new visitor has no idea Tile Redi is the market leader in one-piece systems without navigating to the About page.
  • Competitive framing is absent — no comparison to traditional mud-bed installation or competitor products. This matters enormously for a product that changes a $5,000 trade installation into a DIY project.
  • Quantified benefits are missing. The site says "faster and easier" but doesn't say how much faster (e.g., "30% less installation time vs. mud-bed").
  • The "Since 1998" longevity signal is buried in the About page; it should be visible on the homepage to reduce new visitor skepticism.

💡 Recommendation & Hypothesis

IF the homepage hero section includes a three-part proof statement: "One-piece construction · Any size, any drain location · Leakproof or we make it right," paired with an "Only from Tile Redi" claim, THEN product page add-to-cart rates will increase for homepage-originated sessions, BECAUSE visitors arriving with high-ticket purchase intent need a clear reason to choose Tile Redi over a local supply house or competing brand before investing further engagement.
03
Trust & Credibility
5/10
▾

✅ What's Working

  • A dedicated Testimonials page exists with genuine, detailed customer stories — strong raw material that isn't surfaced on the homepage.
  • Customer service recovery stories in testimonials (replacement pans, next-day shipping) are powerful credibility signals for a high-damage-risk product category.
  • PRO program and commercial site creates legitimacy for contractor buyers.
  • Certifications page exists (UL certification, testing) — excellent trust anchor.

⚠️ Issues Found

  • Recent negative reviews on ConsumerAffairs (shipping delays, no phone response, Dec 2025) directly contradict the homepage trust signals. The shipping delay banner amplifies this anxiety rather than defusing it.
  • No star rating, review count, or social proof widget visible on the homepage or product listing pages (based on available HTML).
  • The phone number (855-455-1167) is in the nav but there is no visible "we answer the phone" guarantee or support hours — critical for a high-consideration product where customers often want to speak to someone before a large purchase.
  • No money-back guarantee, damage protection, or satisfaction pledge visible on the homepage. For a product frequently damaged in shipping, this is a significant anxiety driver.

💡 Recommendation & Hypothesis

IF the homepage features a "Why Buy Direct" trust strip with four icons (UL Certified · Damage Replacement Guarantee · 25 Years Manufacturing · Contractor Approved), placed above the product grid fold, THEN cart abandonment on product pages will decrease, BECAUSE high-ticket, damage-prone purchases require trust scaffolding before intent converts — and current trust signals require navigation to find.
04
CTA & Conversion Path
4/10
▾

✅ What's Working

  • "Find Your Shower Pan" as a top nav item acts as a guided-selling entry point — an underrated conversion asset.
  • PRO-specific sign-up CTA is clearly segmented from consumer path.

⚠️ Issues Found

  • "Shop Now" is the primary homepage CTA — generic, unspecific, and creates no commitment or path clarity for a visitor who doesn't know where to start.
  • No sticky CTA in the header for mobile visitors. The purchase path requires multiple nav interactions before reaching a product.
  • The "Find Your Shower Pan" configurator — likely the highest-converting page on the site — is buried in a nav dropdown rather than featured as a primary homepage CTA.
  • Free shipping offer appears in the header bar but the terms link is a non-functional anchor (#). Unclear terms create purchase anxiety, not incentive.
  • No secondary CTA for lower-intent visitors (e.g., "Download our Installation Guide," "View Measuring Guides") to capture email or advance nurturing.

💡 Recommendation & Hypothesis

IF the primary hero CTA is changed from "Shop Now" to "Find Your Shower Pan →" (linking to the configurator) alongside a secondary "Get PRO Pricing" CTA, THEN configurator engagement will increase and downstream product add-to-cart rates will improve, BECAUSE the configurator self-qualifies visitors by size/drain type, reducing the decision paralysis caused by a catalog with dozens of variants.
05
Product UX / Findability
6/10
▾

✅ What's Working

  • The mega-nav with product images is a strong category-browsing experience — clearly organized by product type.
  • Sub-categories (Redi Base, Redi Trench, Corner, Barrier Free, Base'N Bench) with brief descriptors aid decision-making.
  • Measuring Guides page addresses a top pre-purchase anxiety (will it fit?) — strong friction reducer when discoverable.

⚠️ Issues Found

  • The mega-nav is heavy — five major categories, dozens of sub-items. For mobile, this likely creates a confusing browsing experience.
  • Custom Shower Pans ("Redi Your Way") is a premium, high-ticket differentiator buried as a nav sub-item rather than featured as a distinct pathway for complex projects.
  • No product comparison tool is evident — for a category where shoppers compare point vs. linear drain, corner vs. standard, the absence of comparison functionality likely increases bounce from the product listing pages.
  • The tiles category (new addition) feels grafted onto the site without a clear content rationale on the homepage. It dilutes focus and may confuse first-time visitors about the brand's core identity.

💡 Recommendation & Hypothesis

IF a "Which System Is Right for Me?" quiz/guide is surfaced on the homepage and in the product navigation, THEN sessions per user will increase and products-per-order will improve, BECAUSE shower system purchases typically involve multiple complementary products (pan + wall + drain + door) and guided discovery reduces the cognitive load of self-selection.
06
Content & Persuasion
6/10
▾

✅ What's Working

  • Active blog with relevant, SEO-aligned content (tariffs, installation, new products) — solid top-of-funnel content machine.
  • DIY Gallery mentioned in nav — user-generated content is a powerful bottom-of-funnel trust signal if executed well.
  • Continuing Education / CEU page for contractors demonstrates deep category authority.

⚠️ Issues Found

  • Blog content and social proof (testimonials, DIY gallery) are siloed in the nav and not surfaced on the homepage or product pages where they'd influence purchase decisions.
  • Loss framing is absent. The category's natural anxiety — "what happens if my shower leaks?" — is never addressed with a satisfaction guarantee or warranty language on the homepage.
  • FAQ is nav-buried. Common objections (can I install this myself? what if it arrives damaged? how long does shipping take?) likely drive phone calls or abandonment rather than being resolved inline.

💡 Recommendation & Hypothesis

IF a "Real Projects, Real Results" homepage section surfaces 3 DIY gallery photos with customer quotes and installation times, placed between the product grid and the footer, THEN homepage-to-product-page click-through will increase, BECAUSE social proof placed near a decision point (browsing the site) reduces last-mile hesitation for DIY-segment visitors evaluating whether they can successfully install the product.
07
Mobile Experience
4/10
▾

✅ What's Working

  • Shopify platform provides a reasonable mobile baseline with responsive product grids and native cart behavior.
  • Phone number in the nav is a clickable tel: link — good for mobile conversion.

⚠️ Issues Found

  • The four-layer notification bar stack (shipping delay + promo code + free shipping + PRO signup) eats 25–35% of above-fold mobile viewport before any product content appears. This is a critical mobile-first failure.
  • The mega-nav, while excellent on desktop, likely collapses into a complex hamburger menu on mobile — 5 categories × multiple sub-items creates excessive tap depth to reach products.
  • No evidence of mobile-specific CTAs or sticky "Add to Cart" behavior on product pages. Shopify default may be adequate but warrants testing.
  • Without GA4 data, device conversion gap is unknown — but given notification bar density and nav complexity, mobile conversion is likely meaningfully below desktop.

💡 Recommendation & Hypothesis

IF the mobile header is simplified to a single rotating notification (not four stacked bars), and a sticky "Find My Shower Pan" CTA is added to the mobile homepage footer, THEN mobile scroll depth and configurator entry rate will increase, BECAUSE mobile visitors experiencing above-fold notification overload are more likely to bounce before ever engaging with the product catalog.

48-Hour Action Plan

Five actions to implement immediately, in priority order. None require development resources.

1
Rewrite the shipping delay banner (Day 1, 15 min)
Go into Shopify Admin → Themes → Customize → Announcement bar. Replace the current text with: "Current shipping lead time: [X] business days. Questions? Call 855-455-1167." This directly addresses the #1 trust/anxiety issue on the site without any development work.
2
Fix the free shipping terms link (Day 1, 5 min)
In the same announcement bar settings, link "See Terms" to your actual shipping policy page (/policies/shipping-policy). Removes a micro-anxiety trigger and activates the motivational effect of the free shipping offer.
3
Update the hero CTA copy (Day 1, 10 min)
In the homepage hero section, change the primary CTA from "Shop Now" to "Find Your Shower Pan →" and link to /products/find-your-shower-pan. Also add a one-line sub-headline: "The only one-piece system built to any size, any drain location."
4
Add a 4-icon trust strip (Day 2, 2–4 hrs)
Design and add a simple icon bar section to the homepage (below hero or above product grid): UL Certified | Free Shipping on All Orders | 25 Years Manufacturing | Damage Replacement Guarantee. Use simple SVG icons and brief labels. Can be done in Shopify theme editor with a custom HTML section.
5
Connect GA4 to BigQuery (Day 2, 1 hr setup)
In GA4 → Admin → BigQuery Links, enable the BigQuery export. This enables data-driven prioritization of all future CRO work — device split, bounce by traffic source, funnel drop-off. Without this, all recommendations remain heuristic-only (ICE confidence ceiling of 6).
