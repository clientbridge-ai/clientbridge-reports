Days 1–7 · Phase 1
20 conversations, no pitch
Contact every Tier 1 professional in your existing network — not to sell, to learn. Frame it explicitly: "I'm building something for [industry] firms and want 20 minutes of honest feedback before I launch. No pitch." People love being asked for their opinion. Target: 20 conversations in 7 days. This is your only job this week.

Who to call: former colleagues, clients, referral partners, LinkedIn connections you already have. Houston-first. You need real humans, not survey responses.
Days 8–14 · Phase 2
Identify the hairiest pain
After 20 conversations, pattern-match the answers. You're looking for the pain point that makes people lean forward, interrupt you, or say "yes, that's exactly it." That's your wedge — not the platform, not the workflow library, not the training. The one problem that creates an instant emotional response.

Rewrite your positioning around that pain. If "inconsistent AI usage" isn't making them lean forward but "scaling without hiring" is — pivot to that. Let the market tell you the headline.
Days 15–21 · Phase 3
Sell to 3 people, manually
Take the sharpened positioning and sell it — in person or on Zoom — to the 3 most interested people from your conversations. No automation. No sequences. Just you, the pitch, and a credit card ask at the end. If they won't pay, the hypothesis is broken. If 2 of 3 say yes, you have signal.

Price point: $2,500–$3,500 founding client rate. You're not optimizing for revenue yet — you're testing willingness to pay and validating the problem is real enough to open a wallet.
Days 22–30 · Phase 4
Deliver and measure activation
Start delivering the Sprint for your paying clients. Watch for the one metric that predicts retention: are they logging deliverables daily by week 2? If yes — the product has a hook. If no — you have a delivery or product problem regardless of how good the sale was.

At day 30: debrief every client. Ask the Sean Ellis question directly: "How would you feel if you could no longer use this?" If 40%+ say "very disappointed" — you have PMF. Below 40% — you have work to do.
The 20-minute discovery script
These are in order. Do not pitch. Do not mention H+A until they ask or until the very end. Your job is to listen and take notes verbatim — their exact words become your copy.
1. "Walk me through how your team produces its most important work right now."
Listen for: manual vs. systematic, individual vs. shared, consistent vs. ad hoc. You want them to describe their current state unprompted.
2. "Where does AI show up in that process today — if at all?"
Listen for: which tools, who uses them, how consistently. Are they proud or embarrassed about their answer?
3. "What's the biggest bottleneck in scaling your output without adding headcount?"
This is your money question. The answer tells you what pain is worth paying to solve. Write it down word for word.
4. "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about how your team works, what would it be?"
Ignore the literal answer. Listen for the emotion. Frustration = real pain. Shrug = low urgency.
5. "Have you tried solving this before? What happened?"
Prior attempts = proven pain. Failed attempts = they've already paid to solve it = they'll pay again if you have a better answer.
6. "What would it mean for your business if this was solved?"
This surfaces the value. If they can articulate a dollar figure or a time impact — you have a buyer. If they shrug — you have a browser.
7. (End of call only) "I'm building something that addresses exactly this. Can I show you a quick overview and get your honest reaction?"
This is your only pitch moment. If they say yes — great. If they say no — that's data too. Don't push.
The 3 signals that confirm PMF
You need all three. Two out of three means you're close but not there.
Signal 1 · Language match
Prospects describe their problem using your exact words before you say them. When you hear "we have AI tools but no system" from someone you haven't pitched — that's language match. Your copy is reflecting their reality back at them.
Signal 2 · Inbound pull
People start referring you without being asked. A discovery conversation ends with "you should talk to my partner" or "can I forward this to someone?" Organic referral within the first 30 days is one of the strongest early PMF signals that exists.
Signal 3 · Fast close
The sales conversation is short. Objections are light. People say "how do I get started" before you've finished the pitch. If you're fighting hard to close every deal — the positioning or the ICP is wrong. PMF feels like pushing a door that's already open.

Day 30
Be ruthless. Set your kill criteria before you start.
The biggest PMF mistake founders make is moving the goalposts. Decide now what failure looks like — so you don't rationalize your way past it in week 3.
Kill signal 1
Fewer than 5 of 20 conversations express genuine pain around AI inconsistency or scaling. If the problem isn't resonating broadly — you're solving the wrong problem or talking to the wrong people.
Kill signal 2
Zero of 3 pilot pitches convert to a paid engagement. If nobody will pay $2,500 for a clear, demonstrated solution — the pain isn't acute enough or the trust isn't there yet.
Kill signal 3
Paying clients don't activate — they're not logging deliverables by week 2. Product works but nobody uses it = delivery problem that kills retention and referrals regardless of how good the sale was.
Pivot option 1 — wrong ICP
If conversations light up in one vertical but go flat in others — go all-in on the one that's working. Don't spread. If accounting firms are leaning in and insurance agencies aren't — you have your answer.
Pivot option 2 — wrong pain hook
If "AI consistency" doesn't land but "scale without hiring" does — rewrite every message around that. The platform is the same. The entry point changes. Test a new hook for 2 weeks before concluding the offer doesn't work.
Pivot option 3 — wrong delivery model
If people love the idea but balk at the Sprint format — test a lighter entry: a 1-day AI workflow workshop at $997. Lower barrier, faster yes, still gets them into the platform. Upsell the Sprint from there.
The founder's rule
You don't have a marketing problem until you have a PMF problem solved. No sequence, no landing page, no content strategy fixes a broken hypothesis. Do the 20 conversations first. Everything else waits.
