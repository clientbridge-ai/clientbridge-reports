01 — Company Overview

What MandataMD Does

MandataMD integrates directly into an organization's EHR, allowing providers to order non-prescription products for patients in a single click at the point of care. The patient receives an SMS with a prefilled cart, confirms their order, and receives direct delivery — all triggered before they leave the exam room.

The platform solves a specific, quantifiable problem: non-Rx revenue leakage. When providers verbally recommend OTC products, 20–30% of patients don't follow through, 10–15% select the wrong product, and the majority who do purchase go to Amazon or retail — none of which benefits the health system.

MandataMD's current focus verticals: Hospitals, Orthopedic Centers, Pain Management, Physical Therapy, Research Centers, and Gastroenterology.

02 — ICP Analysis

Who Buys This — And Why Right Now

MandataMD's most receptive buyers are mid-size to large health systems and specialty practices navigating three simultaneous pressures: shrinking reimbursement margins, patient retention pressure, and increasing operational scrutiny over every revenue line. The non-Rx gap is invisible on most P&Ls — which makes it both a problem and an opportunity.

"Revenue leakage occurs when healthcare organizations fail to collect the full reimbursement they are entitled to — and most of it is preventable."

— Morgan Hunter Healthcare, March 2026

The highest-urgency buyers are in three scenarios: (1) practices that recently expanded visit volume without a corresponding revenue-per-visit increase, (2) orthopedic and pain management groups that recommend OTC products at nearly every visit but have no fulfillment infrastructure, and (3) health systems that added GI or physical therapy service lines in the last 12–18 months and are still optimizing those workflows.

Persona: Chief Medical Officer / VP Medical Operations
Responsible for both clinical quality and operational efficiency. Feels the tension between physician time constraints and adherence outcomes. Knows patients are buying the wrong thing at CVS but has no scalable fix. Responds to data showing per-visit revenue impact and workflow simplicity — not feature lists.

Secondary Persona: Revenue Integrity / CFO
Focused on capturing revenue that's already being generated but not collected. Sees non-Rx as an unaddressed line item. Responds to quantified per-visit leakage estimates tied to their actual visit volume.

Gap Category Root Cause Estimated Cost
Patient non-adherence 20–30% don't purchase recommended products $1.0M–$1.5M
Wrong product selection 10–15% choose ineffective OTC alternatives $350K–$900K

04 — Pain-Qualified Segments (PQS)

Finding Practices at Exactly the Right Moment

Rather than targeting all orthopedic groups or all hospital systems, the highest-conversion targeting identifies organizations at specific, verifiable moments when the non-Rx gap is most acutely felt — and when a decision-maker is most likely to act.

PQS

Scenario 1 — Orthopedic Groups Expanding Volume Without New Revenue Lines

Data Recipe: Indeed/LinkedIn job postings (front desk, PT aide) + Google Maps new location filings + CMS provider enrollment changes

Signal: Orthopedic or PT practice posted 3+ clinical support roles in the last 60 days AND opened or announced a new location. Volume is growing; revenue infrastructure hasn't caught up. Every new visit is an OTC recommendation without a capture mechanism.

Subject: The revenue gap that grows with your new location Saw your new Centennial clinic and the PT aide postings — looks like you're scaling fast. One thing I've noticed across practices adding visit volume: non-Rx product recommendations increase proportionally, but fulfillment infrastructure doesn't. Our data suggests ~$38 in recoverable revenue per orthopedic visit that currently walks out the door to Amazon or CVS. Worth a 20-minute look at what that adds up to at your current volume?

Hook: acknowledges a publicly visible growth event, quantifies the gap they don't know they have, asks for a conversation anchored to their specific math.

PQS

Scenario 2 — GI Groups Seeing High No-Show / Repeat Visit Rates

Data Recipe: Healthgrades/Google reviews mentioning follow-up visits or product confusion + Indeed postings for patient care coordinators + recent insurance contract announcements

Signal: GI practice has recent patient reviews mentioning being "told to buy X" at the pharmacy with no follow-through, AND is actively hiring patient care coordinators (a signal of adherence/coordination gaps). GI patients receive non-Rx dietary supplements, prep kits, and bowel management products at nearly every visit.

Subject: Your patients are getting the wrong prep products Noticed a few reviews on your Austin location mentioning patients confused about which products to buy after their consult — pretty common in GI. The practices fixing this aren't doing more patient education. They're removing the decision entirely: provider orders the exact product in the EHR, patient gets a text with a prefilled cart before they leave the building. Two practices in your area went from ~12% OTC adherence to near 100% in 90 days. Want the specifics?

Hook: mirrors a verifiable, specific frustration visible in public data. Positions the solution as removing friction, not adding it.

PQS

Scenario 3 — Pain Management Clinics Under Margin Pressure Post-Reimbursement Changes

Data Recipe: CMS reimbursement change announcements (2025 Physician Fee Schedule) + clinic job postings referencing "revenue cycle" or "billing" + Becker's / local health system news on margin initiatives

Signal: Pain management group posted a revenue cycle or billing coordinator role in the last 90 days, signaling active margin scrutiny. CMS 2025 fee schedule cuts affected pain management reimbursement — groups are actively looking for non-reimbursement revenue. Non-Rx topicals (Voltaren, Biofreeze, custom compounded) are recommended at the majority of visits.

Subject: Non-Rx revenue while reimbursements contract With CMS fee schedule changes hitting pain management this year, a lot of groups are looking at what revenue they can capture outside of insurance. One line item that's consistently overlooked: non-prescription products. Your providers are already recommending them — topicals, supplements, therapy aids. The issue is where that revenue lands (usually Amazon). Quick question: do you have any visibility into what percentage of your OTC recommendations actually convert to a purchase through your practice?

Hook: connects a verifiable industry event (CMS fee changes) to a specific revenue gap, ends with a curiosity-driving question they almost certainly can't answer.

05 — Permissionless Value Propositions (PVP)

Delivering Intelligence Before Asking for Anything

These outreach concepts deliver standalone, useful intelligence to the prospect — information they would find valuable even if they never responded. The act of receiving the insight creates context for the conversation MandataMD wants to have.

PVP

Concept 1 — Per-Visit Non-Rx Leakage Calculator (Orthopedics)

Data Recipe: CMS Provider Utilization data (public visit volume by NPI) + MandataMD internal leakage model ($38–$65/visit for orthopedic OTC categories)

Value delivered: A practice-specific dollar estimate of annual non-Rx revenue leakage based on their actual reported visit volume — information they've never seen quantified for their own practice.

Subject: Estimated $847K in non-Rx revenue leaving Steadman Clinic annually Based on CMS utilization data, your Vail location processed approximately 22,400 orthopedic visits last year. Using conservative OTC adherence benchmarks (30% follow-through, $38 average non-Rx order), that's roughly $847,000 in non-Rx product revenue that's currently going to retail or Amazon — not your practice. No ask attached to this. If the number looks interesting, happy to walk through where it comes from.

The number is so specific it demands a response even if only to challenge it. The "no ask attached" framing disarms the sales guard immediately.

PVP

Concept 2 — OTC Adherence Benchmark Report for GI Practices

Data Recipe: Published GI adherence research (colonoscopy prep compliance rates) + MandataMD platform data on OTC order completion rates + specialty-specific OTC category volume estimates

Value delivered: A 1-page benchmark showing what OTC product adherence looks like in GI practices pre- and post-EHR-integrated ordering, alongside the specific product categories where the gap is largest (colonoscopy prep, dietary supplements, bowel management).

Subject: GI non-Rx adherence data — your specialty specifically Put together a one-pager on OTC adherence patterns in GI practices — thought it might be useful context for your team. Short version: verbal recommendations convert at roughly 28% in GI. EHR-triggered SMS ordering runs at 91%+. The gap is largest in colonoscopy prep products and post-procedure dietary supplements. Worth sharing with your ops team? Happy to send the full version.

Delivers a useful benchmark they can share internally. The ask is low-friction ("worth sharing?") and positions MandataMD as a data-informed partner.

PVP

Concept 3 — Competitive Intelligence on Health System Non-Rx Programs

Data Recipe: Health system press releases + LinkedIn announcements + Becker's/Modern Healthcare news monitoring for "patient retail," "non-prescription," "OTC program" announcements by competitors in the target's region

Value delivered: Intelligence on what a regional competitor just announced regarding a non-Rx patient product program — framing urgency without manufacturing it. Delivered to a CMO or VP Ops at a competing system.

Subject: Intermountain just launched a non-Rx patient ordering program Intermountain Health announced a point-of-care non-prescription ordering program last month — providers order via EHR, patients receive product directly, bypassing retail entirely. Worth knowing, particularly if you're in overlapping service areas. Happy to send you the announcement and the approach they took if that's useful context for your own planning.

Delivers competitive intelligence the prospect genuinely values. Creates urgency without fabricating it — the competitor announcement is real and verifiable.

PVP

Concept 4 — Physical Therapy Practice Amazon Leakage Snapshot

Data Recipe: Amazon Best Sellers in PT/recovery categories (TENS units, resistance bands, cold therapy, topical analgesics) + estimated PT visit volume from CMS NPI data + average OTC recommendation rate for PT visits

Value delivered: A short snapshot showing what PT patients are actually buying on Amazon after their visits — with the specific products and volumes — making the leakage concrete and retail-anchored rather than abstract.

Subject: What your patients are buying on Amazon after their PT visit Pulled Amazon's top 10 best sellers in the PT recovery and home exercise category for the past 90 days — TENS units, resistance bands, Biofreeze, cold therapy wraps. The overlap with standard PT provider recommendations is nearly complete. Based on your visit volume, a conservative estimate puts your annual Amazon-diverted OTC revenue at $340K–$580K. Happy to share the full product list and math if useful.

Makes the leakage problem visible through a familiar lens (Amazon) rather than abstract revenue terminology. The "math" offer closes with natural curiosity.

06 — Strategic Framework

The Data Moat MandataMD Can Build

The targeting approach above creates compounding advantages that generic outreach cannot replicate:

Competitive Advantages of Precision Targeting

  • Unique Targeting Angles: While competitors send the same healthcare SaaS email templates, MandataMD arrives with practice-specific leakage estimates, specialty-calibrated benchmarks, and real competitor intelligence. The prospect has already received value before the first call.
  • Timing Precision: Expansion job postings, reimbursement change windows, and new location filings create narrow windows of maximum receptivity. Reaching a decision-maker 30 days after they opened a new location vs. 18 months later is a fundamentally different conversation.
  • Barrier to Replication: Synthesizing CMS utilization data, Amazon category data, and public review signals into a practice-specific number takes real work. Competitors won't do it. That effort differential is the moat.
  • Data Flywheel: Each PVP sent generates response data — which specialties engage, which leakage estimates land hardest, which trigger events produce the fastest responses. The targeting system improves with every send.
  • Vertical Depth: Orthopedics, PT, pain management, and GI each have distinct OTC product categories, adherence patterns, and trigger events. Segment-specific messaging outperforms horizontal healthcare outreach by 3–5x in response rate.

The long-term advantage: as MandataMD builds platform data on OTC order completion rates, product category performance, and adherence benchmarks across its customer base, those benchmarks become increasingly proprietary and difficult to replicate — creating a compounding data asset that makes future outreach even more specific and compelling.
